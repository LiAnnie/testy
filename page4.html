Short Stories

 

     Story 1

               "Emma? Darling, I'm home." I called, walking into my apartment. My daughter was
          nowhere to be seen. She usually ran up to me whenever I came home, but maybe she was
          sleeping.
          I checked her room, but she wasn't there. "Emma, are you here?" I called again. No answer.
          I was starting to get worried.
               I repeatedly called her name as I searched through all the rooms and hiding places.
          Finally, I got to the closet. I opened the door and let out a huge sigh of relief when I saw
          her eyes staring back at me from the top shelf.
               "There you are! Why didn't you answer?"
               She just kept staring back at me.
               "Here, let's get you down," I said, reaching for her. The feeling of her skin shocked me.
          It wasn't the usual feeling. Instead, her skin felt icy cold and stiff.
               "Emma?"
               She hadn't moved an inch. I felt her neck for a pulse, but there was none. Only when
          I looked back at her face, did I realize that her normally hazel eyes were black, just like
          her mother's were when she died.
 

 

     Story 2

               Outside, the snowstorm raged. The polar bear family stayed inside their small igloo.           The baby, Siku, pranced around inside, waving an icicle like a sword.
               “I’ll defeat you, you monster!” She yelled in glee. “Evil shall fall!” Siku jumped           around on the snow furniture when suddenly, she tripped and fell to the ground. She           looked at what she had tripped over and was confused. She had never seen the object           before. It wasn’t made from snow, however, it shined as brightly as snow.
               “Siku, honey, be careful!” Her mother called from the kitchen.
               Siku jumped. “Of course, mommy.”
               She studied the strange object for a while and then picked it up. It was a golden ball           with embedded jewels on it. The object started glowing. Siku’s eyes widened in           amazement. She held up the ball and realized her arm was also glowing. She looked           down and found out it wasn’t just her arm, but her whole body.
               “Whoa!” She cried. “Mommy, look at-” but before she could finish her sentence, she           was whisked up by the wind.
               A cloud caught her and slowly started drifting away from her igloo. They were above           the storm clouds, although she could still see the small icy hut and the three stone bears.           Siku was whisked farther and farther out by the cloud and she was becoming scared.
               “Put me down! I want to go home!” She cried.
               The wind stopped and the cloud dispersed. Siku was dropped into the deep ocean. As           she struggled to stay afloat, Siku wondered how it had happened. How did the shining           object move her? She struggled to paddle to the closest iceberg and then lay her head           on the cold surface.
               “I can’t get home.” She sniffled. “I can’t swim!” After a while of thinking, Siku           came to a realization.
               “I can swim!” She said excitedly. “I just swam here from the ocean!”
               She jumped into the water and started paddling to the next closest iceberg. “I think
          I can do it. No, I know I can do it!”
               It was hard to swim back by herself, and along the way, Siku often had to stop for a           long time, but as she swam more, she began needing fewer breaks. Eventually, she got to           shore.
               “I did it!” Siku exclaimed. “I’m almost home!”
               On the cliff in front of her, she could see the three stone bears. Siku knew it was
          the way back home because of her ride out. Using her small claws, she climbed up the           obstacle and bounded across the snowy terrain.
               “I’m almost home! I’m almost home!” She yelled.
               She hopped to the small valley where her family lived and shuffled through the snow,           up the small hill. Then she rolled down into her house. Her father looked up from the           snow television.
               “Siku, where have you been? Dinner’s almost ready.” He said.
               She smiled. “I went on an adventure.”
 

 

     Story 3

               There was once an old man, named Clark, who lived in a small shack. Every day, he           would go to the park to watch the birds, and then walk to the park, where he would spend           ages working on his wooden boat. On this day however, when he went to the park, he           noticed some eyes watching him from the bushes.
               "Hey! Out with ya'!"
               There was a rustle of leaves and a dog peeked out the top of the bush. It looked           scruffy and weak. Clark was appalled by its appearance. He was so terrified that the           monster would rip him to shreds as soon as he turned his back, that he got up           immediately and walked backwards all the way home. The dog followed him closely.           No matter how Clark tried to get rid of him, he just wouldn't leave.
               When Clark closed his door, the dog sat by the entrance, waiting for him to come           back out. Clark tried his hardest to stay in the house, but after two days, he felt nauseous.           Clark grabbed his cane and opened the door, swinging at the dog. It jumped back, but           still would not go away. Clark shook his head and went to the park. Instead of watching           the birds, he just stared at the dog, who in turn, stared back.
               After a few days living like this, the dog started growing on him. Instead of seeing           the monster he used to see, he now saw a poor stray.
               "What's up, dog? What you doin'?"
               The dog got on his stomach and put his head down. He whimpered.
               "You hungry?"
               The dog barked once.
               "Alright." Clark went into the house and got the dog some food. The dog ate it happily.           From then on, Clark took care of the dog. He bathed it and fed it, and it brought him joy.
               One day, Clark decided to bring the dog inside. He spent all his money getting the dog           everything it needed, from a bowl to a bed. He even installed a dog door. In the night,           Clark woke up sweating. Something felt wrong. Maybe it was the feeling of not being           lonely. He rolled around and went back to sleep.
               In the morning, Clark went down the stairs. He got to the dog's room, but it was gone.           All the food, the bed, and the supplies were gone. The room was a mess. Clark sank to           his knees. He got cheated by a dog.
